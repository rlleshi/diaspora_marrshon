# Mission

## Purpose

This website exists to turn diaspora attention into confirmed, peaceful commitments to march for Albania.

The public site should persuade people to commit. Private coordination channels should organize people only after they have confirmed their intent and consented to data use for follow-up.

## Primary Goal

The first version optimizes for one clear action:

**I pledge to march for Albania.**

Every major page, call to action, and workflow should support that goal before expanding into broader campaign, press, or logistics features.

The march frame is:

**Diaspora marshon ne Tirane: from Tirana International Airport "Nene Tereza" toward Skanderbeg Square / Sheshi Skenderbej.**

## Audience

- Albanian diaspora members who may arrive in Albania and join a peaceful march.
- Local organizers who need reliable pledge counts and route interest.
- City coordinators who need filtered, verified lists for follow-up.
- Volunteers who can help with stewardship, translation, media, legal support, logistics, or accessibility.
- International allies, journalists, and civic groups who need a clear explanation of the civic action.

## Mission Principles

- Peaceful civic action only.
- The march happens only under the Albanian national flag.
- The march does not represent any political party, organization, or interest group.
- Support the public interest, Albania's future, protected nature, and cultural heritage.
- Support transparency, accountability, public consultation, and rule-of-law process.
- Respect every participant's political, religious, and social views.
- Reject insults, threats, provocation, discrimination, and incitement to violence.
- Use Albanian as the primary language from the start, with English as the secondary language.
- Collect the minimum personal data needed for coordination.
- Treat participant safety and anti-spam controls as core product requirements.
- Never publish raw participant lists, private phone numbers, or public WhatsApp invite links.

Vjosa-Narta and protected nature may remain part of the campaign context, but Albania itself is the pledge target.

## Success Criteria

The site is successful when it can reliably answer:

- How many people have confirmed a march pledge?
- Where are pledges coming from?
- Which route, joining point, or participation type did people choose?
- Which people opted into volunteer follow-up?
- Which confirmed participants can be safely handed to trusted coordinators?

Public vanity metrics are secondary. Verified commitments matter more than noisy signup volume.

## Coordination Model

The website is the commitment layer.

Email confirmation is the verification layer.

Trusted coordinators and private messaging groups are the coordination layer.

WhatsApp or similar group links must not be posted publicly. They should be sent only after signup, consent, email confirmation, and any required manual coordinator review.

## Public Message

The site should communicate that the diaspora is not only speaking from abroad. The diaspora is willing to take concrete, disciplined, peaceful action for the homeland.

The core website voice should be Albanian-first. English translations should help diaspora members, international allies, and media follow the message, but English should not drive the primary tone or structure.

Core message:

**We come from different countries, but when Albania calls, we answer.**

The march is not against ordinary citizens or against different political beliefs. It is a call for public responsibility, protection of the national interest, protection of nature and cultural heritage, and the future of the country.

## Civic Demands Context

The website may explain that the march supports citizen demands including:

- Government resignation.
- Repeal of the strategic investor status and related legal framework.
- Repeal of the Mountains Package.
- Reversal of changes to protected-areas law.
- Reversal of changes to cultural-heritage law.

These demands should be presented as campaign context, while the signup flow remains focused on the peaceful diaspora march for Albania.

## Out Of Scope For The First Version

- Public social network features.
- Open comments or forums.
- Public participant maps with personal data.
- Automatic public bus organization.
- Publicly visible private group links.
- Complex campaign CMS features before the pledge flow works.
